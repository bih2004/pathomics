Digital Slide Scanning Services

High-resolution whole slide imaging for remote viewing, sharing, and advanced digital analysis

Overview

Digital slide scanning, also known as whole slide imaging (WSI), is the process of converting traditional glass slides into high-resolution digital images that can be viewed, shared, and analyzed using specialized software. This technology has revolutionized pathology by enabling remote consultation, digital archiving, educational applications, and advanced computational analysis.

At Pathomics, our digital slide scanning services utilize state-of-the-art scanners and software to provide high-quality digital slides with exceptional resolution and color fidelity. We offer rapid turnaround times, secure data storage options, and seamless integration with our imaging analysis services for comprehensive digital pathology solutions.

Our Digital Slide Scanning Services Include

  • Brightfield Scanning: High-resolution scanning of H&E and IHC-stained slides.
  • Fluorescence Scanning: Multi-channel fluorescence scanning with spectral unmixing capabilities.
  • Z-Stack Imaging: Multi-plane scanning for thick sections and cytology specimens.
  • Batch Scanning: High-throughput scanning of large slide sets with consistent quality.
  • TMA Scanning: Specialized scanning of tissue microarrays with core identification.
  • Digital Slide Management: Secure storage, organization, and retrieval of digital slides.
  • Image Sharing: Secure platforms for sharing digital slides with collaborators.
  • Quality Control: Rigorous quality assessment of all scanned images.
  • Format Conversion: Conversion between various digital slide formats.
  • Integration with Analysis: Seamless connection to our imaging analysis services.

Slide Requirements

To ensure optimal results from our digital slide scanning services, please follow these guidelines:

  • Slide Type: Standard 1" x 3" (25mm x 75mm) glass slides with frosted ends.
  • Coverslipping: Slides should be properly coverslipped with appropriate mounting media.
  • Labeling: Slides should be clearly labeled with unique identifiers.
  • Slide Quality: Slides should be clean, free of air bubbles, and have good staining quality.
  • Section Thickness: Optimal section thickness is 4-5 Î¼m for most applications.
  • Slide Orientation: Tissue should be properly oriented and centered on the slide.
  • Special Requirements: Please notify us of any special requirements (e.g., fluorescence, Z-stack) in advance.

For specific slide preparation requirements or questions, please contact our laboratory.
