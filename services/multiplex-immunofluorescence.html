Multiplex Immunofluorescence Services

Simultaneous detection of multiple biomarkers in a single tissue section with spatial context

Overview

Multiplex immunofluorescence (mIF) is an advanced technique that allows for the simultaneous detection and visualization of multiple biomarkers within a single tissue section. This powerful approach provides critical spatial context and cellular relationships that are essential for understanding complex tissue microenvironments, particularly in cancer research and immuno-oncology applications.

At Pathomics, our multiplex immunofluorescence services utilize cutting-edge technology and optimized protocols to deliver high-quality, reproducible results with exceptional multiplexing capabilities. Our expert team can detect up to 8 biomarkers simultaneously on a single tissue section, providing unprecedented insights into cellular interactions, tissue architecture, and biomarker co-expression patterns.

Our Multiplex Immunofluorescence Services Include

  • High-Plex Immunofluorescence: Simultaneous detection of up to 8 biomarkers in a single tissue section.
  • Tyramide Signal Amplification (TSA): Enhanced sensitivity for detection of low-abundance antigens.
  • Spectral Unmixing: Advanced spectral imaging and unmixing for accurate separation of fluorescent signals.
  • Multispectral Imaging: High-resolution multispectral image acquisition for detailed analysis.
  • Spatial Analysis: Comprehensive spatial analysis of cellular interactions and tissue architecture.
  • Custom Panel Design: Development of custom antibody panels tailored to your specific research questions.
  • Quantitative Analysis: Advanced image analysis for objective quantification of biomarker expression and cellular phenotyping.
  • Expert Interpretation: Detailed interpretation of multiplex immunofluorescence results by experienced pathologists.

Multiplex Immunofluorescence Technologies

We utilize several cutting-edge technologies for multiplex immunofluorescence:

Tyramide Signal Amplification (TSA)

TSA technology enables sequential staining of multiple antibodies raised in the same species by covalently depositing fluorophores onto tissue and allowing for complete antibody stripping between rounds. This approach provides exceptional sensitivity and multiplexing capabilities.

Multispectral Imaging

Our multispectral imaging systems capture the complete spectral profile at each pixel, allowing for accurate separation of overlapping fluorescent signals and autofluorescence removal. This technology enables reliable detection of multiple biomarkers in a single tissue section.

Spectral Unmixing

Advanced spectral unmixing algorithms separate individual fluorescent signals based on their unique spectral profiles, enabling clear visualization and quantification of each biomarker even with spectral overlap.

Automated Image Analysis

Our sophisticated image analysis platforms provide cell segmentation, phenotyping, and spatial analysis capabilities, allowing for objective quantification of biomarker expression and cellular interactions.

Sample Requirements

To ensure optimal results from our multiplex immunofluorescence services, please follow these sample preparation guidelines:

  • Fixation: Tissues should be fixed in 10% neutral buffered formalin (preferred) for 24-48 hours.
  • Processing: Standard paraffin embedding is preferred for most applications.
  • Section Thickness: Optimal section thickness is 4-5 μm for most applications.
  • Slide Type: Positively charged slides are required for multiplex immunofluorescence.
  • Storage: Freshly cut slides are preferred; if storage is necessary, store at 4°C (short-term) or -20°C (long-term).
  • Controls: Please provide appropriate positive and negative control tissues when available.

For specific sample preparation requirements or questions, please contact our laboratory.
