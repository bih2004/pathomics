Immunohistochemistry Services

Precise detection and visualization of specific antigens in tissue sections with validated antibodies

Overview

Immunohistochemistry (IHC) is a powerful technique that combines anatomical, immunological, and biochemical methods to identify and visualize specific tissue components through antigen-antibody interactions. At Pathomics, we offer comprehensive IHC services that detect specific antigens in tissue sections using validated antibodies, providing crucial insights into protein expression, localization, and distribution.

Our expert team utilizes state-of-the-art equipment and optimized protocols to deliver high-quality, reproducible IHC results for research, diagnostic, and pharmaceutical applications. We offer both single and multiplex IHC with quantitative analysis for biomarker expression in various tissue types, supporting your specific research and clinical needs.

Our Immunohistochemistry Services Include

  • Single Antigen IHC: Detection and visualization of individual antigens in tissue sections.
  • Multiplex IHC: Simultaneous detection of multiple antigens in a single tissue section.
  • Chromogenic IHC: Visualization using chromogenic substrates (DAB, AEC, etc.) for brightfield microscopy.
  • Fluorescent IHC: Visualization using fluorescent dyes for fluorescence microscopy.
  • Automated IHC: High-throughput, standardized staining using automated platforms.
  • Custom Protocol Development: Optimization of staining protocols for specific antibodies and tissue types.
  • Quantitative Analysis: Digital image analysis for objective quantification of biomarker expression.
  • Expert Interpretation: Pathologist review and interpretation of IHC results.

Multiplex IHC Capabilities

Our multiplex IHC services allow for the simultaneous detection of multiple antigens in a single tissue section, providing valuable insights into the spatial relationships between different cell types and biomarkers.

Benefits of Multiplex IHC

  • Visualization of multiple biomarkers in the same tissue section
  • Preservation of tissue architecture and spatial context
  • Reduced sample requirements compared to serial section staining
  • Enhanced understanding of complex cellular interactions
  • More comprehensive data from limited tissue samples

Multiplex IHC Approaches

  • Sequential Multiplex IHC: Multiple rounds of staining on the same tissue section with chromogenic detection.
  • Fluorescent Multiplex IHC: Simultaneous detection of multiple antigens using different fluorophores.
  • Tyramide Signal Amplification (TSA): Enhanced sensitivity for detection of low-abundance antigens in multiplex assays.

Quantitative Analysis

We offer comprehensive quantitative analysis of IHC staining using advanced digital pathology tools:

Sample Requirements

To ensure optimal results from our immunohistochemistry services, please follow these sample preparation guidelines:

  • Fixation: Tissues should be fixed in 10% neutral buffered formalin (preferred) or other appropriate fixative.
  • Fixation Time: Optimal fixation time is 24-48 hours for standard samples.
  • Sample Size: Tissue samples should be no larger than 1.5 x 1.5 x 0.3 cm for optimal fixation.
  • Tissue Processing: Standard paraffin embedding is preferred for most applications.
  • Slide Preparation: If providing pre-cut slides, use positively charged slides and appropriate section thickness (typically 4-5 μm).
  • Storage: Store blocks at room temperature and cut slides at 4°C (short-term) or -20°C (long-term).

For specific sample preparation requirements or questions, please contact our laboratory.
