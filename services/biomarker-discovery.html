Biomarker Discovery

Comprehensive approaches for identifying and validating novel biomarkers for research, diagnostic, and therapeutic applications

Overview

Biomarkers are measurable indicators of biological processes, pathogenic processes, or responses to therapeutic interventions. The discovery and validation of novel biomarkers is essential for advancing precision medicine, developing new diagnostics, and identifying therapeutic targets. At Pathomics, our comprehensive biomarker discovery services combine advanced tissue analysis technologies with sophisticated computational approaches to identify, characterize, and validate biomarkers with clinical and research significance.

Our multidisciplinary team of pathologists, molecular biologists, and data scientists employs a systematic approach to biomarker discovery, leveraging our expertise in histology, immunohistochemistry, multiplex imaging, digital pathology, and artificial intelligence. Whether you are seeking diagnostic, prognostic, predictive, or pharmacodynamic biomarkers, our integrated discovery platform can help you identify and validate biomarkers that address your specific research or clinical needs.

Our Biomarker Discovery Services Include

  • Tissue-Based Biomarker Discovery: Identification of protein, RNA, and morphological biomarkers in tissue samples.
  • Multiplex Biomarker Profiling: Simultaneous analysis of multiple biomarkers to identify significant patterns and correlations.
  • Spatial Biomarker Analysis: Assessment of spatial relationships and tissue context in biomarker expression.
  • AI-Driven Biomarker Identification: Application of machine learning to discover novel biomarkers and patterns.
  • Biomarker Validation: Rigorous validation of candidate biomarkers across multiple samples and cohorts.
  • Translational Biomarker Development: Development of biomarkers with clinical utility and implementation potential.
  • Custom Assay Development: Creation of specialized assays for novel biomarker detection and quantification.
  • Multimodal Data Integration: Correlation of imaging biomarkers with molecular and clinical data.
  • Biomarker Performance Assessment: Evaluation of sensitivity, specificity, and clinical utility of biomarkers.
  • Companion Diagnostic Development: Creation of biomarker assays for therapeutic selection and monitoring.

Biomarker Types

We discover and validate various types of biomarkers to address diverse research and clinical needs:

Diagnostic Biomarkers

Markers that aid in disease detection and diagnosis, enabling accurate classification and differentiation of disease states.

Prognostic Biomarkers

Indicators that provide information about disease outcome and progression independent of treatment.

Predictive Biomarkers

Markers that forecast the likelihood of response to specific treatments, guiding therapeutic selection.

Pharmacodynamic Biomarkers

Indicators that demonstrate target engagement and biological response to therapeutic interventions.

Monitoring Biomarkers

Markers that track disease status, progression, or treatment response over time.

Safety Biomarkers

Indicators that detect or predict adverse effects of treatments or interventions.

Susceptibility/Risk Biomarkers

Markers that identify individuals with increased likelihood of developing specific diseases.

Spatial Biomarkers

Patterns of cellular distribution and interaction that provide insights into disease mechanisms and outcomes.

Validation Approaches

We employ rigorous validation strategies to ensure biomarker reliability:

  • Technical Validation: Assessment of assay reproducibility, precision, and robustness.
  • Biological Validation: Confirmation of biological relevance and mechanism of action.
  • Clinical Validation: Evaluation of association with clinical outcomes and parameters.
  • Independent Cohort Validation: Testing in separate, independent sample sets.
  • Cross-Platform Validation: Confirmation using different detection methods and platforms.
  • Multivariate Analysis: Assessment of biomarker performance in combination with other markers and clinical factors.
  • Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC) Analysis: Rigorous statistical evaluation of sensitivity and specificity.
  • Survival Analysis: Assessment of association with patient outcomes using Kaplan-Meier and Cox regression methods.
