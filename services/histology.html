Histology Services

Comprehensive tissue processing, sectioning, and staining services with expert interpretation

Overview

Histology is the study of the microscopic structure of tissues. At Pathomics, our histology services provide the foundation for all tissue-based research and diagnostic applications. We offer comprehensive tissue processing, embedding, sectioning, and staining services with a wide range of histochemical stains to meet your specific research and clinical needs.

Our team of experienced histotechnologists and pathologists ensures high-quality tissue preparations with rapid turnaround times and expert interpretation. We work with various tissue types, including human, animal, and plant tissues, and can accommodate both routine and specialized histology requests.

Our Histology Services Include

  • Tissue Processing: Fixation, dehydration, clearing, and paraffin infiltration of tissues using state-of-the-art automated tissue processors.
  • Tissue Embedding: Precise orientation and embedding of tissues in paraffin blocks for optimal sectioning.
  • Tissue Sectioning: High-quality sections at various thicknesses (2-20 μm) using advanced microtomes.
  • Routine Histochemical Staining: Hematoxylin and Eosin (H&E), the gold standard for tissue morphology assessment.
  • Special Stains: Comprehensive range of special stains for specific tissue components and pathological changes.
  • Frozen Sections: Rapid processing and staining of frozen tissue sections for time-sensitive applications.
  • Digital Slide Scanning: High-resolution scanning of stained slides for digital analysis and remote consultation.
  • Expert Interpretation: Pathologist review and interpretation of histological findings.

Special Stains Available

We offer a comprehensive range of special stains to visualize specific tissue components and pathological changes:

Connective Tissue Stains

  • Masson's Trichrome (collagen fibers)
  • Verhoeff-Van Gieson (elastic fibers)
  • Picrosirius Red (collagen types)
  • Alcian Blue (mucins, glycosaminoglycans)
  • Periodic Acid-Schiff (PAS) (glycogen, basement membranes)

Microorganism Stains

  • Gram Stain (bacteria)
  • Ziehl-Neelsen (acid-fast bacteria)
  • Grocott's Methenamine Silver (GMS) (fungi)
  • Warthin-Starry (spirochetes)
  • Giemsa (parasites)

Pigment and Mineral Stains

  • Perl's Prussian Blue (iron)
  • Von Kossa (calcium)
  • Congo Red (amyloid)
  • Fontana-Masson (melanin)
  • Alizarin Red (calcium)

Neurological Stains

  • Luxol Fast Blue (myelin)
  • Bielschowsky Silver (neuronal elements)
  • Nissl (neuronal cell bodies)
  • Bodian (neuronal elements)
  • Golgi (neuronal morphology)

Our Histology Process

1

Sample Receipt

Tissue samples are received, logged, and assessed for quality and adequacy.

2

Tissue Processing

Tissues undergo fixation, dehydration, clearing, and paraffin infiltration.

3

Embedding

Processed tissues are precisely oriented and embedded in paraffin blocks.

4

Sectioning

Thin sections (typically 4-5 μm) are cut from the paraffin blocks and mounted on glass slides.

5

Staining

Slides are stained with appropriate histochemical stains based on the research or diagnostic requirements.

6

Quality Control

All slides undergo rigorous quality control to ensure optimal staining and tissue morphology.

7

Digital Scanning

Slides can be digitally scanned for remote viewing and analysis (optional).

8

Interpretation

Expert pathologists review and interpret the histological findings (optional).

Sample Requirements

To ensure optimal results from our histology services, please follow these sample preparation guidelines:

  • Fixation: Tissues should be fixed in 10% neutral buffered formalin or other appropriate fixative.
  • Fixation Time: Optimal fixation time depends on tissue size (typically 24-48 hours for standard samples).
  • Sample Size: Tissue samples should be no larger than 1.5 x 1.5 x 0.3 cm for optimal fixation and processing.
  • Labeling: All samples must be clearly labeled with unique identifiers.
  • Documentation: Complete submission forms with relevant clinical or experimental information.

For specific sample preparation requirements or questions, please contact our laboratory.
