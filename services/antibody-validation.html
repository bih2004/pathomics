Antibody Validation for IHC

Comprehensive validation services ensuring specificity, sensitivity, and reproducibility of antibodies for immunohistochemistry applications

Overview

Antibody validation is a critical step in ensuring the reliability and reproducibility of immunohistochemistry (IHC) results. At Pathomics, we provide comprehensive antibody validation services to verify the specificity, sensitivity, and reproducibility of antibodies across various tissue types and fixation conditions.

Our expert team employs a multi-tiered validation approach that follows industry best practices and guidelines to ensure that antibodies perform consistently and generate reliable results for your research and clinical applications. Whether you are developing a new antibody, validating an existing one for a new application, or establishing a clinical IHC assay, our validation services provide the confidence you need in your antibody's performance.

Our Antibody Validation Services Include

  • Specificity Testing: Comprehensive assessment of antibody specificity using multiple validation strategies.
  • Sensitivity Evaluation: Determination of optimal antibody concentration and detection limits.
  • Reproducibility Testing: Assessment of staining consistency across multiple runs and conditions.
  • Protocol Optimization: Development of optimal staining protocols for specific antibodies and applications.
  • Cross-Reactivity Assessment: Evaluation of potential cross-reactivity with similar epitopes.
  • Fixation Compatibility: Testing antibody performance across different fixation methods and conditions.
  • Tissue Microarray Validation: High-throughput validation across multiple tissue types using TMA technology.
  • Quantitative Analysis: Objective measurement of staining intensity and distribution.
  • Validation Reports: Comprehensive documentation of validation methods and results.

Validation Strategies

We employ multiple validation strategies to ensure comprehensive assessment of antibody performance:

Genetic Strategies

  • Knockout/Knockdown Validation: Comparison of staining in tissues with and without target gene expression.
  • Orthogonal Validation: Correlation of protein expression with mRNA levels or other measurement methods.
  • Expression Modulation: Analysis of staining in tissues with experimentally modulated target expression.

Analytical Strategies

  • Western Blot Correlation: Comparison of IHC results with Western blot analysis.
  • Mass Spectrometry Correlation: Verification of target protein identity using mass spectrometry.
  • Independent Antibody Verification: Comparison of staining patterns using multiple antibodies against different epitopes of the same protein.

Tissue-Based Strategies

  • Positive/Negative Tissue Controls: Validation using tissues with known expression patterns.
  • Tissue Microarray Screening: High-throughput validation across multiple tissue types.
  • Subcellular Localization: Verification of expected subcellular staining patterns.

Technical Validation

  • Titration Studies: Determination of optimal antibody concentration.
  • Antigen Retrieval Optimization: Assessment of various antigen retrieval methods.
  • Fixation Studies: Evaluation of antibody performance across different fixation conditions.
  • Reproducibility Testing: Assessment of inter-run and inter-operator consistency.

Antibody Submission Requirements

To ensure optimal results from our antibody validation services, please provide the following:

  • Antibody Information: Source, clone, lot number, and target information.
  • Antibody Quantity: Minimum of 100 μL at working concentration or 25 μg of purified antibody.
  • Target Information: Details about the target protein, including gene name, accession number, and epitope (if known).
  • Existing Protocols: Any available staining protocols or previous validation data.
  • Application Information: Specific intended applications and tissue types of interest.
  • Positive Control Tissues: Known positive control tissues or cell lines (if available).

For specific submission requirements or questions, please contact our laboratory.
