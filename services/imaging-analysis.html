Imaging Analysis Services

Advanced quantitative analysis of pathology images

Advanced quantitative analysis of digital pathology images using state-of-the-art algorithms and machine learning

Overview

Digital pathology imaging analysis transforms qualitative visual assessments into objective, quantitative measurements, enabling more precise and reproducible evaluation of tissue samples. At Pathomics, our advanced imaging analysis services utilize state-of-the-art algorithms and machine learning approaches to quantify biomarker expression, cell populations, and tissue architecture from digital pathology images with high accuracy and reproducibility.

Our expert team combines deep pathology knowledge with computational expertise to deliver tailored analysis solutions for research, pharmaceutical development, and clinical applications. Whether you need basic quantification of immunohistochemistry staining or complex spatial analysis of the tumor microenvironment, our comprehensive imaging analysis services provide the insights you need to advance your research and development efforts.

Our Imaging Analysis Services Include

  • Biomarker Quantification: Precise measurement of biomarker expression in tissue sections.
  • Cell Detection and Counting: Automated identification and enumeration of specific cell populations.
  • Tissue Segmentation: Delineation of different tissue compartments and regions of interest.
  • Spatial Analysis: Assessment of spatial relationships between different cell populations.
  • Multiplex Analysis: Quantification of multiple biomarkers in single tissue sections.
  • Tumor Microenvironment Analysis: Comprehensive characterization of the tumor-immune microenvironment.
  • Morphometric Analysis: Quantification of cellular and tissue morphological features.
  • Custom Algorithm Development: Creation of tailored analysis solutions for specific research questions.
  • Machine Learning Applications: Advanced pattern recognition and classification using AI approaches.
  • Comprehensive Reporting: Detailed analysis reports with visualizations and statistical summaries.

Analysis Capabilities

We offer a comprehensive range of analysis capabilities to address diverse research needs:

IHC Quantification

Precise measurement of chromogenic immunohistochemistry staining, including:

  • Positive cell counting and percentage calculation
  • Staining intensity measurement (H-score, Allred score)
  • Membrane, cytoplasmic, and nuclear staining assessment
  • Region-specific quantification
  • Threshold-based and AI-based approaches

Multiplex Analysis

Comprehensive analysis of multiplex immunofluorescence and immunohistochemistry, including:

  • Multi-marker phenotyping
  • Co-expression analysis
  • Cell population quantification
  • Spatial distribution mapping
  • Cell-cell interaction assessment

Tissue Architecture Analysis

Detailed assessment of tissue structure and organization, including:

  • Tissue segmentation (tumor, stroma, necrosis)
  • Tumor margin delineation
  • Invasive front identification
  • Vascular density measurement
  • Architectural pattern recognition

Spatial Analysis

Advanced spatial relationship assessment, including:

  • Nearest neighbor analysis
  • Clustering and dispersion metrics
  • Spatial correlation statistics
  • Distance mapping
  • Interaction zone identification

Morphometric Analysis

Quantification of cellular and tissue morphological features, including:

  • Nuclear size, shape, and texture
  • Cellular pleomorphism assessment
  • Glandular architecture evaluation
  • Tissue pattern recognition
  • Morphological feature extraction

Machine Learning Applications

Advanced AI-based analysis approaches, including:

  • Supervised classification
  • Unsupervised clustering
  • Deep learning-based segmentation
  • Feature extraction and selection
  • Predictive modeling

Data Requirements

To ensure optimal results from our imaging analysis services, please provide:

  • Digital Slides: High-quality whole slide images in standard formats (SVS, TIFF, JPEG2000, etc.).
  • Staining Information: Details about staining methods, antibodies, and protocols used.
  • Analysis Objectives: Clear description of analysis goals and specific parameters of interest.
  • Sample Information: Relevant metadata about tissue samples and experimental conditions.
  • Group Assignments: Information about sample grouping for comparative analyses.
  • Reference Images: Examples of expected staining patterns or regions of interest (if available).

For specific data submission requirements or questions, please contact our laboratory.
