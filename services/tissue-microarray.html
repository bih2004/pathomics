Tissue Microarray Services

High-throughput analysis of multiple tissue samples on a single slide for efficient biomarker screening and validation

Overview

Tissue Microarray (TMA) technology enables the simultaneous analysis of multiple tissue samples on a single microscope slide. This powerful approach dramatically increases throughput while reducing reagent consumption, time, and cost compared to conventional methods that analyze one tissue section per slide.

At Pathomics, our comprehensive TMA services include custom TMA design, construction, sectioning, and staining for efficient biomarker screening and validation. Our expert team utilizes state-of-the-art equipment and optimized protocols to deliver high-quality TMAs that meet your specific research and clinical needs.

Our Tissue Microarray Services Include

  • Custom TMA Design: Tailored TMA design based on your specific research questions and sample availability.
  • TMA Construction: Precise extraction and arraying of tissue cores using advanced automated TMA instruments.
  • TMA Sectioning: High-quality sectioning of TMA blocks for consistent results across multiple slides.
  • Histochemical Staining: Standard and special stains for tissue morphology assessment.
  • Immunohistochemistry: Single and multiplex IHC for biomarker detection and analysis.
  • Immunofluorescence: Single and multiplex immunofluorescence for enhanced multiplexing capabilities.
  • Digital Slide Scanning: High-resolution scanning of TMA slides for digital analysis.
  • Image Analysis: Automated quantification of biomarker expression across multiple tissue cores.
  • Data Management: Comprehensive tracking and organization of TMA data.
  • Expert Interpretation: Pathologist review and interpretation of TMA results.

Types of Tissue Microarrays

We offer several types of TMAs to meet your specific research needs:

Multi-Tumor TMAs

Arrays containing cores from multiple tumor types for broad screening applications and comparative studies.

Tumor Progression TMAs

Arrays containing tissues representing different stages of tumor progression (normal, pre-malignant, primary tumor, metastasis) to study disease evolution.

Case-Control TMAs

Arrays containing matched tumor and normal tissues from the same patients for direct comparison studies.

Outcome TMAs

Arrays containing tissues from patients with known clinical outcomes for prognostic biomarker studies.

Treatment Response TMAs

Arrays containing tissues from patients with known treatment responses for predictive biomarker studies.

Normal Tissue TMAs

Arrays containing various normal tissue types for biomarker specificity assessment and toxicity studies.

Cell Line TMAs

Arrays containing cell line pellets with known molecular characteristics for biomarker validation and assay development.

Custom TMAs

Arrays designed and constructed according to your specific research requirements.

TMA Design Considerations

Our expert team will help you design an optimal TMA for your specific research needs, considering the following factors:

  • Core Diameter: Selection of appropriate core size (typically 0.6-2.0 mm) based on tissue heterogeneity and available material.
  • Core Number: Determination of optimal number of cores per case to account for tumor heterogeneity and ensure representative sampling.
  • Array Size: Design of array dimensions based on the number of cases and cores per case.
  • Control Inclusion: Strategic placement of positive and negative control tissues within the array.
  • Orientation Markers: Incorporation of orientation markers for proper slide alignment and core identification.
  • Replicate Cores: Inclusion of replicate cores from the same case to enhance statistical power and account for tissue loss.
  • Randomization: Consideration of randomized core placement to minimize position-dependent staining artifacts.

Sample Requirements

To ensure optimal results from our tissue microarray services, please follow these sample preparation guidelines:

  • Fixation: Tissues should be fixed in 10% neutral buffered formalin (preferred) or other appropriate fixative.
  • Processing: Standard paraffin embedding is required for TMA construction.
  • Block Quality: Donor blocks should contain sufficient tissue depth (at least 1 mm) for core extraction.
  • H&E Slides: Representative H&E slides should accompany each donor block for pathologist review.
  • Documentation: Complete submission forms with relevant clinical or experimental information.

For specific sample preparation requirements or questions, please contact our laboratory.
